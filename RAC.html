RCA Records

One of the most storied names in the music industry, traces its origins to the dawn of recorded sound and has played a pivotal role in shaping the soundtracks of generations. From the invention of the 45 rpm single to launching the careers of music icons, RCA’s journey is intertwined with the history of music itself.

1900s

1901-1929: The Birth of an Empire

The story of RCA begins in 1901, with the founding of the Victor Talking Machine Company by Eldridge R. Johnson. This pioneering company revolutionized music consumption, manufacturing phonographs and records that brought music into homes across America.

1920s

In 1929, Radio Corporation of America (RCA) acquired Victor, forming RCA Victor. This merger combined radio and recording, setting the stage for a new era of influence.

1930s

Innovation in the Face of Adversity

The 1930s saw RCA Victor lead sound innovation during the Great Depression, introducing the 33⅓ rpm format, which later became a standard.

2020s

RCA Records remains a powerhouse with acts like Doja Cat, Khalid, and H.E.R., thriving in the streaming era while honoring its legacy as a pioneer of recorded sound.
